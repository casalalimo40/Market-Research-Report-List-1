<p><ul><li>Mechanical Micropipettes</li><li>Electronic Micropipettes</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The micropipette market is primarily divided into two types: mechanical and electronic micropipettes. Mechanical micropipettes rely on a manual plunger mechanism for liquid handling, offering simplicity and cost-effectiveness, making them ideal for basic applications. In contrast, electronic micropipettes utilize automated controls for precise dispensing, reducing user fatigue and ensuring greater accuracy, particularly in complex workflows. Both types are essential in laboratories for tasks such as DNA amplification, cell culture, and various assays, catering to diverse user needs and preferences.</p></p>
